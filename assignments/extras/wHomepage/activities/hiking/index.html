Hiking

When it comes to trails we all know that there are different types and degrees of hiking trails, especially in the appalacian mountain range, so heres a list of different trails in Williamsport or near that you can go to.

Blue Bird Trail- WMWA Water Shed

Difficulty: Easy, Length: 2.4 Miles, Rating: 4.4/5

The Blue Bird Trail is in a little area owned by the Williamsport Municipal Water Authority, which also houses their watersheds. This trail is one of the many trails that they own and they say to be the easiest out of them all, this trail's shape is a loop, and takes about 52 mins if you go at a slow and walkable pace. While you hike thos trail you can see multiple informational signs that show different foundations or plots of land that were used by multiple things and made a little "Town". The most recent addition to these trails were the informational signs, made by Chris Decker, and updated by Gabriel Carson, and the WMWA for their Eagle Scout Project.

* For More information visit the Williamsports Municipal Water Authroities Website for the lands, and History of the locations. *











Susquehanna River Walk and Timber Trail- Williamsport, PA

Difficulty: Easy, Length: 11.0 Miles, Rating: 4.4/5

The Susquehanna River Walk and Timber Trail is located in the City of willaismport and stretches to near bouroghs of South Williamsport across the Susquehanna river giving it its name. This trail is also relatively easy because the ground tends to be level and many families use it to walk and enjoy the sights with beautiful sunsets, and beautiful forests surrounding the trail as well as the river. On this trail you can also see historical signs of Williasmport and also informational signs about some plants local to Williasmport, and also some local animals.

* For more information about the trail visit Susquehanna Green Way. *











Raccoon Mountain- Williasmport

Difficulty: Moderate, Length: 6.2, Rating: 4.4/5

The Raccoon Mountain Trail is also located in the same area owned by the Williamsport Municipal Water Authority, which is near/where the Blue Bird Trail. This trail is also a loop like path but also has a higher altittude compared to the Blue Bird Trail, which makes it moderately difficult even though it may be shorter. This trail also houses some History that you can also see on the Blue Bird Trail twoards the end of it. At a part of the trail you are actually able to look off of a vista, which helps overlook the valley, and with this trail it is very well maintained and has beautiful greenery and animals that you can see.

* For more information about the trail visit Williamsports Municipal Water Authroities Website for the information of the lands, and History of the locations. *











Smiths Knob Trail- Loyalsock State forest

Difficulty: Hard, Length: 4.8, Rating: 4.6/5

The Smith Knob trail is located in the Loyalsock State Park which is a local park about an hour away from Williamsport. This trail is considered a difficult trail because of how much of elevation gain there is compared to the other trails in this list, even though the trail is only 4.8 miles, it is said that you could finish it in around 2 hours or more. When it comes to this trail no matter what time it is when going on it you can get beautiful views especially with the sunset and sunrise, where you overlook a valley. Since it is a state trail these paths are kept pretty nice but due to it being apart of the Appalacian Trail its is only one part of many trails.

* For more information about the trail visit All Trails for more information, photos, and maps of the trail. *
